FINTECH breaks Banks

FINTECH breaks Banks

Viktor Remsha, General partner of FinSight Ventures and Chairman of the Board of Directors of FINAM group, spoke about how new technologies in the field of Finance are changing the format of consumption and how to make money from it.

All the new technologies that are coming to the financial sphere are developing in a landscape that has been formed for many years. This former formation is built on certain business principles and has its own shortcomings and vulnerabilities. In developed countries, the scale of the banking business is such that the price of an error is sufficient it is very large, so it is difficult for banks to change. Of course, innovations still appear, banks are trying to catch up with a certain technological lag, but the issue is not just about technology. Banks are used to earning money on certain items, it is not easy for them to decide on steps that can lead to shortfall profit for shareholders. But banks cannot ignore the rapid arrival of new technology companies on the market, which are changing the landscape of the financial world. Banks are learning something from FINTECH, they are still competing with it in some areas, and they are forced to cooperate in certain areas.

First the trend concerns the transactional part of the banking business, which is now being taken over by FINTECH . New companies bring high-tech products to the market at low cost and win back a significant part of the business. For example, the Chinese ones WeChat and Alipay processed about $ 3 trillion in payments in 2016, compared with $ 80 billion in 2012. The cost of these payment systems, of course, has also grown significantly. WeChat is owned by Tencent, a Hong Kong-based company exchange: Mcap (market capitalization) at the beginning of 2016 was $ 180.89 billion, at the beginning of 2017 — already $ 231.4 billion, and in December 2017- $ 487.6 billion (data as of 19.12.2017). Alipay is a payment system of the giant Alibaba Group, whose shares are listed on the New York stock exchange (NYSE): Mcap at the beginning of 2016 — $ 192.67 billion, at the beginning of 2017 - $ 219.19 billion, by the end of the year-already $ 438.67 billion.

The situation is similar with American public companies PayPal and Square — they "pick up" the banking transaction business and increase their capitalization. PayPal is traded on NASDAQ, the company's market capitalization at the beginning of 2016 was $ 42.45 billion, at the beginning of 2017 — $ 48.56 billion, by the end of 2017 - $ 89.54 billion. In the PayPal system, more than 5 million transactions are made daily, and for the quarter — 1.9 billion. Total volume transactions for the quarter amount to $ 114 billion. The growth is also reflected in the dynamics of revenue: 2015 — $ 9.248 billion, 2016 - $ 10.842 billion, forecast for 2017 - $ 12.981 billion, in 2018, revenue is expected to reach $ 15.239 billion.

Market capitalization of Square, whose shares trading on the NYSE, at the beginning of 2016, was at the level of $ 4.07 billion, at the beginning of 2017, it was $ 4.86 billion, and by December it had jumped several times — to $ 14.35 billion. At the moment, the processing speed of Square's own transactions is 4.2 seconds. Total transaction volume for Q3 2017 — $ 17.4 billion (compared to the 3rd quarter of 2016- $ 13.2 billion). In 2017, the company expects a profit of $ 108.59 million, and the forecast for 2018 is $ 204.61 million, for 2019 - $ 349.48, for 2020 - $ 541.43 million, for 2021 - $ 684.70.
